# Humanizer (AI Trace Remover)

**Humanizer** is a professional writing editor skill designed to identify and remove obvious signs of AI-generated text. Based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" guide, it makes writing sound more natural and human by eliminating over-polishing, stereotypical patterns, and empty persuasive rhetoric.

## Tags

🗂️ Documents & Office | ✅ Verified

## Core Philosophy

- **De-AI-fication**: Identifies and rewrites common AI "verbal tics" (e.g., "Moreover," "In summary"), overused punctuation (e.g., em dashes —), and hollow generalized descriptions.
- **Injecting Soul**: Great writing is more than just cleaning up slop; it requires injecting specific opinions, varied rhythms, and real human emotion, moving away from uniform, perfect statements.
- **Concrete Over Abstract**: Replaces AI-favored phrases like "a pivotal milestone" with specific actions like "resolved the XX issue in the XX project," reclaiming reader trust through detail.

## Key Features & Workflow

1. **AI Pattern Scanning**: Automatically detects 24 typical AI signs, including inflated symbolism, false lists, and the "rule of three" (over-reliance on three-part structures).
2. **Natural Rewriting**: Transforms stiff "-ing" endings and verbose transition phrases into concise, strong, and idiomatic expressions.
3. **Rhythm & Emotion Tuning**: Adjusts sentence length distribution and adds a first-person perspective (where appropriate) to shift copy from "Wikipedia style" to "Human communication style."
4. **Validation & Comparison**: Provides before-and-after comparisons and explanations of editing strategies, showing how to distill "AI slop" into valuable real information.

## Skills Library Overview

- **Pattern Library**: Built-in metrics for 24 language and grammar-level AI patterns.
- **Rewriting Guide**: Covers content patterns (e.g., undue emphasis on significance), language patterns (AI high-frequency vocabulary), and style patterns (e.g., excessive bolding).
- **Soul Injection**: Advanced editing strategies specifically for enhancing the readability and authenticity of the manuscript.
