# Renwei Writing

**Renwei Writing** is an AI writing skill for polishing, editing, and rewriting text while preserving the presence and authenticity of the person behind the words.

## Tags

🗂️ Documents & Office | ✅ Verified

## Core Philosophy

- **Preserve human presence**: The goal of editing is not to make the text more polished, but to ensure the reader can still feel the real person behind it after the edits.
- **Subtraction first, edit less**: Every change must be justified by a real obstacle in the original text; if you cannot explain it, do not change it.
- **Respect the author's fingerprints**: Redundancy, colloquialisms, uneven parallelism, and filler particles should first be treated as the author's voice, not flaws.

## Key Features & Workflow

1. **Trigger recognition**: Automatically activates when users ask to "help me edit this," "polish this," "rewrite this," or complain that the text "feels too AI" or "lacks human flavor."
2. **Three-layer human-flavor assessment**: Understand the original text through stance (who is speaking), cost (whether real experience was paid), and fingerprints (personal expression habits).
3. **Seven editing rules**: Subtract rather than add, preserve tone particles, avoid crafting golden sentences, prefer plain description when uncertain, explain every change, and more.
4. **Post-edit checklist**: Scan only the sentences you changed against `post-edit-checklist.md` for AI tells such as em dashes, rule-of-three lists, and "not X but Y" structures.
5. **Failure case study**: Includes a full three-round polishing log with original, botched, and final versions side by side to calibrate against the common trap of over-editing.

## Skills Library Overview

- **Core principles**: `SKILL.md` — defines the three elements of human flavor, editing rules, and gotchas learned from real failures.
- **Post-edit checklist**: `references/post-edit-checklist.md` — scan your own edits for AI patterns, adapted from Wikipedia *Signs of AI writing* (via blader/humanizer, MIT) for Chinese writing.
- **Case study**: `references/case-study.md` — a full failure autopsy with original, botched, and final versions side by side.
